Induction cooktops have become a go‑to for home cooks who want speed, safety, and efficiency without the fuss of gas or electric coils. Because they heat the pan directly, they boil water faster, respond instantly to temperature changes, and stay cool to the touch. The catch? You need ferromagnetic cookware (a simple magnet test will tell you). When choosing an induction cooktop, consider whether you want a portable single burner for countertop use or a built‑in model that becomes part of your kitchen. For portables, look at power settings (most range from low simmer to high boil), temperature precision, and pan size compatibility. For built‑in units, assess burner layout, total power, and smart features. Our round‑up covers both types to help you match the right cooktop to your cooking habits.
